My laptop is Windows 10, whose biggest failing IMHO is the lack of a centralised software management. You may wonder why I haven't installed Linux, the answer is Microsoft 365 day to day I need it (although I suppose I could us the online version). Fortunately Windows Subsystem for Linux (WSL) is available on insider preview channel.

Much simpler when all that's required is `sudo apt-get update` or `pacman -Syu`.

It's like a role reversal - 15-20 years ago one of the biggest hassles in Linux was dependency hell. Then RPM came along and things progressively improved.

Windows is very stagnant as an operating system. This is only anecdotal but I've noticed fewer people around me using laptops and an increasing use of iPads or often just a phone.
